# Code of Conduct

OpenMCAS exists for the benefit of people with MCAS / MCAD and the
researchers who help them. Everything below derives from that.

## Standards

We expect everyone — contributors, maintainers, patients, clinicians,
academics, industry visitors, journalists — to:

- **Lead with the patient.** This isn't an abstract exercise. People are
  sick and waiting. Decisions that trade off rigor for hype, or hype for
  paralysis, both hurt them.
- **Be honest about uncertainty.** Hypothesis ≠ evidence ≠ treatment.
  Use the evidence-level tags. Publish negative results.
- **Be specific in criticism.** "This ranking is wrong" is unhelpful.
  "Cetirizine should rank above hydroxyzine because hERG…" is helpful.
- **Be patient with non-experts.** Patients without medical training
  may ask questions that sound naïve to a researcher. Their questions
  are exactly why this repo exists. Answer in plain English.
- **Be patient with experts.** Researchers may push back on patient-
  reported patterns. Their pushback is exactly why this repo exists.
  Show the data.

## Unacceptable

- Medical advice in issues, PRs, or commit messages. This includes
  "you should try X" comments to other patients. Use [docs/disclaimers.md](docs/disclaimers.md)
  as the model.
- Personal attacks, harassment, doxxing, dismissiveness based on
  identity or condition.
- Soliciting or sharing personal health information (PHI). De-identified
  case observations are welcome; identifiable data is not.
- Promotion of commercial products as treatments.
- Patent-encumbered contributions or attempts to acquire IP rights to
  publicly-posted compounds.
- Bad-faith engagement designed to slow the project (pharma astroturf,
  pseudoscience promotion, etc.).

## Enforcement

Maintainers may close issues, reject PRs, lock threads, and as a last
resort block contributors who violate this code repeatedly or egregiously.
Decisions will be posted publicly with the rationale.

Disagreements about an enforcement decision can be raised by opening an
issue tagged `governance` and tagging the maintainer team.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness conduct that violates this code, open an
issue tagged `code-of-conduct` (or for sensitive reports, request a
private channel by opening an issue and asking us to reach out off-list).

We will not retaliate against good-faith reporting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all OpenMCAS project spaces — repository,
issues, PRs, discussions, related Discord / forum / chat (if any),
events, and any other space the project explicitly endorses.
