# Wet-lab protocol references

This doc points to **established, published assays** that an academic or CRO
mast cell lab can run to validate hypotheses generated by this pipeline. None of
the protocols are invented here — we are linking to standard methods.

> ⚠️ **Not medical advice.** Research protocols only — for use by trained
> laboratory personnel under IRB / IBC oversight where applicable.

## Cell systems

- **LAD2 cells** — human mast cell line derived from a mastocytosis patient;
  expresses functional FcεRI and MRGPRX2. Sourced from NIH NIAID under MTA.
- **HMC-1** — human mast cell leukemia line; expresses KIT D816V; useful for
  clonal-MCAS modeling.
- **CD34⁺-derived human mast cells** — gold standard but slow + expensive
  (8–12 weeks differentiation).
- **iPSC-derived mast cells** — emerging; patient-specific.
- **Primary skin / lung mast cells** — from human surgical samples; closest to
  in vivo, hardest to access.

## Core readouts

### β-hexosaminidase release (degranulation)
Cells are stimulated (substance P / anti-IgE / Compound 48/80 / Ca²⁺ ionophore).
Supernatant β-hex activity vs. total lysate activity = % degranulation. Standard
plate-based fluorometric or colorimetric assay (4-methylumbelliferyl-2-acetamido-2-deoxy-β-D-glucopyranoside substrate).

### Histamine ELISA
Direct quantification in supernatant. Multiple commercial kits.

### Tryptase ELISA
Mast-cell-specific marker. Both total + mature tryptase kits available.

### Cytokine multiplex (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β, IL-8, TSLP, etc.)
24h supernatant after sub-stimulating triggers. Reads out late-phase
inflammatory response.

### CD63 surface expression (flow cytometry)
Activation marker translocated to surface during degranulation. Useful for
flow-cytometric quantification of % activated cells.

### Calcium flux (Fluo-4 / Fura-2)
Real-time degranulation upstream signal. Plate reader or imaging.

### Nrf2-axis readouts (for SFN / remission hypotheses)
HMOX1 and NQO1 mRNA (qPCR) or HO-1 protein (Western); nuclear Nrf2
translocation by ICC.

## Simulated injury panels (linked to `MCAS_Injury_Mechanisms_v1.csv`)

| Injury axis | Stimulus | Pre-treatment compound to test |
|---|---|---|
| Oxidative | H₂O₂ 100–200 µM | SFN, NAC, α-lipoic, EGCG |
| MRGPRX2 / chemical | Substance P 5–20 µM; Compound 48/80 | Resveratrol, quercetin, luteolin, cromolyn |
| IgE / FcεRI | Biotinylated IgE + anti-biotin / streptavidin crosslink | Quercetin, luteolin, cromolyn, ketotifen |
| Gut / endotoxin | LPS 100 ng/mL – 1 µg/mL | Butyrate, curcumin, baicalein |
| Clonal / KIT | SCF stimulation in KIT+ lines | Masitinib, avapritinib, midostaurin |

## Suggested validation cascade

1. **Primary screen (single dose):** Each AI-ranked candidate (top 20 per
   category from `outputs/ranked_*.csv`) at 10 µM on LAD2 + each injury panel.
   Readout: β-hex release.
2. **Dose-response:** 7-point curve on actives (IC₅₀ + max inhibition).
   Cromolyn + quercetin as reference compounds.
3. **Orthogonal confirmation:** Tryptase ELISA + CD63 flow on top 5.
4. **Mechanism:** Cytokine multiplex + Nrf2 readouts to confirm proposed axis.
5. **Cytotoxicity:** MTT / Cell-Titer-Glo to rule out toxicity-driven "stabilization."
