# For nonprofits, patient-advocacy orgs, and foundations

You're funding or organizing around MCAS / MCAD / mast-cell disease. You
want grant dollars and patient hours to produce durable, public, audit-able
benefit — not patents, not press releases, not closed datasets.

This is the open hypothesis infrastructure that's *missing* from MCAS
research. We built it. We need partners to operate, validate, and scale it.

> ⚠️ This repo does not provide medical advice. See
> [docs/disclaimers.md](../docs/disclaimers.md).

## Who you'd be partnering with

**MR Dula Medical** (a DBA of **MR Dula Enterprise, LLC**), Raleigh, NC, USA.
We are an independent, MIT-licensed open-research project. Not affiliated
with pharma, not VC-backed, not currently a 501(c)(3) (see below).

## What we are (and aren't)

We **are**:
- An MIT-licensed public hypothesis-generation pipeline for MCAS.
- A reproducible AI + cheminformatics pipeline that anyone can run on a laptop.
- A standardized publishing format for in silico and wet-lab experiments.
- A neutral coordination layer between patients, clinicians, academia, and
  industry — with no IP capture and no paywall.

We **are not**:
- A 501(c)(3) (yet).
- A clinical-care provider.
- A drug developer.
- A patent holder.
- Funded by pharma or industry.

## Where nonprofit support has the most leverage

In order of estimated impact-per-dollar:

### 1. Wet-lab validation campaigns ($5k–$25k each)
The top-10 rankings in `hypotheses/{rescue,maintenance,remission}.md` are
in silico hypotheses. To turn any into evidence we need β-hex / CD63 /
tryptase release assays on LAD2 cells (or primary mast cells) against
simulated injury panels. Estimated cost per campaign: $5–25k via an
academic mast-cell lab or CRO. Every campaign produces a published
experiment report + open data + a refined ranking.

**What a sponsor gets:** Named credit in the [`experiments/`](../experiments/)
report and the resulting publication. Open, reproducible evidence
attached to a public DOI. No conflicts of interest.

### 2. Patient-reported data infrastructure ($10k–$40k)
We capture trigger patterns and compound-response patterns via GitHub
issues today. A proper structured-intake form (privacy-preserving, no
PHI, optional clinician validation) would let us collect 10–100× the
signal. Build cost is modest; ongoing operations are mostly volunteer.

### 3. Compute for AI experiments ($1k–$10k / year)
The local CPU pipeline runs in minutes. The Colab-GPU experiments
(REINVENT 4, DiffDock, ESM-based) are where real model improvements live.
Modest cloud-credit underwriting unblocks experiments that currently
require contributors to pay out of pocket.

### 4. Coordination + outreach
Volunteer time to:
- Translate hypothesis docs into patient-readable summaries.
- Connect us with mast-cell labs willing to validate.
- Bring patient communities into the trigger/response data layer
  without crossing into medical-advice territory.

## What a partnership looks like

We will gladly:
- Co-brand experiment reports with sponsor logos.
- Provide quarterly transparency reports on what was funded, what was
  spent, and what was learned.
- Co-author press releases on validated findings.
- Co-write grants (NIH / NIAID / HHMI / Mastocytosis Society) where the
  nonprofit is the fiscal sponsor.
- Provide a public ledger of nonprofit contributions (financial,
  in-kind, volunteer hours).

We will refuse:
- Exclusive partnerships.
- Embargoed results.
- "Champions" arrangements that would constrain editorial independence
  of the rankings.
- Patent assignment.
